Brazil

Brazil is South America’s largest country, rich in culture, landscapes, and ecosystems. The Amazon Rainforest, the world’s largest tropical forest, where I was born, covers about 40% of Brazil’s territory, with nearly 60% of the entire rainforest lying inside Brazil. It is a cradle of biodiversity and a vital regulator of Earth’s climate. Alongside it once stood the Atlantic Forest, stretching along Brazil’s coast; today, less than 15% of its original area remains due to centuries of deforestation. Despite these challenges, Brazil’s natural and cultural diversity—from vibrant cities to unique ecosystems—remains one of the country’s greatest treasures, shaping its beauty and global identity.
